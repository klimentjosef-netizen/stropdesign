## Traditional drywall or modern stretch ceiling?

If you're considering a suspended ceiling, you're probably choosing between drywall and a stretch ceiling. Both solutions have their place, but in many respects stretch ceilings clearly come out on top. Let's look at a detailed comparison.

## Speed and cleanliness of installation

A drywall ceiling requires several stages: frame assembly, screwing in boards, filling, sanding and painting. The entire process takes several days and leaves a lot of dust and mess in the room.

**A stretch ceiling is installed in half a day**, without dust and without having to move furniture. Profiles are attached around the perimeter of the room, the film is heated and stretched. Done. No filling, no sanding.

## Durability and maintenance

This is where stretch ceilings clearly win:

- **Waterproof**: the film withstands water, moisture and chlorine. Ideal for bathrooms, kitchens and pool halls.
- **Crack-free**: unlike drywall, which cracks at joints over time, a stretch ceiling holds its shape throughout its entire lifespan.
- **Easy maintenance**: just wipe with a damp cloth a few times a year. No repainting needed.

## Design options

Drywall offers a smooth white surface and the option of shaping (cascades, steps). But stretch ceilings go much further:

- **8 surface types**: matte, satin, glossy, metallic, translucent, custom print, perforated, acoustic
- **Hundreds of colors**: from classic white to bold shades
- **Custom prints**: photographs, patterns, sky — anything you can imagine
- **Integrated LED lighting**: strips, spot lights, design lines directly in the ceiling
