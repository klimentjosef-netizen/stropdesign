## Lighting is the key to atmosphere

A stretch ceiling without well-planned lighting is like a car without an engine. It looks nice, but you're not using its full potential. Integrating LED lighting directly into the ceiling is one of the biggest advantages of stretch ceilings over traditional solutions.

## Types of lighting for stretch ceilings

### 1. Spot lights

The most common choice for even room lighting. Recessed spot lights are nearly invisible and provide direct light exactly where you need it.

**Ideal for:** kitchens, bathrooms, hallways, offices

**Tip:** Plan approximately 1 spot per 1.5–2 m². Above kitchen work surfaces, you can increase the density.

### 2. LED strips

Continuous lines of light around the perimeter or in the centre of the ceiling. LED strips create soft, indirect lighting that is ideal as ambient light.

**Ideal for:** living rooms, bedrooms, wellness areas

**Tip:** Choose LED strips with adjustable colour temperature (warm/cool). You'll appreciate warm light in the evening and cool light when working.

### 3. Design LED lines

Geometric shapes (lines, squares, diamonds) created from LED profiles under the stretch ceiling. They function as both main lighting and a design element.

**Ideal for:** modern interiors, hallways, representative spaces

### 4. Translucent ceiling backlighting

Translucent film with LED panels behind it creates the effect of an evenly lit surface. The result resembles a giant light panel or a glowing window to the sky.

**Ideal for:** bathrooms, waiting rooms, wellness, luxury interiors

### 5. Starry sky

Optical fibres threaded through the film simulate a starry sky. A romantic and relaxing element loved by children and adults alike.

**Ideal for:** bedrooms, children's rooms, home cinemas

## How to combine multiple types

The best results come from combining:

- **Spot lights** for functional lighting (reading, cooking)
- **LED strips** for ambient atmosphere (evening relaxation)
- **Design lines** as the main visual element

Each circuit should be on a separate switch or dimmer so you can switch between modes.

## What to watch out for

- **Colour temperature**: for homes we recommend 2700–3000 K (warm white). For offices 4000 K (neutral white).
- **Dimmability**: invest in dimmable sources. The price difference is minimal, but the comfort is huge.
- **Power supply**: LED sources need a transformer (driver). Make sure there's enough space above the ceiling.
- **Light positioning**: plan the layout before ceiling installation. Subsequent changes are possible but more complicated.
