## Bathrooms are a challenge for any ceiling

High humidity, hot steam, direct contact with water. A bathroom is an environment where ordinary ceiling solutions quickly give up. Paint yellows and peels, drywall swells and develops mould. And this is precisely where stretch ceilings excel.

## Why choose a stretch ceiling for your bathroom?

### Absolute waterproofing

The PVC film of a stretch ceiling is **100% waterproof**. It withstands steam, moisture and direct contact with water. It can even catch water in the event of a leak from the flat above: the film bulges but doesn't let a single drop through.

### No mould

Unlike drywall or traditional plaster, mould doesn't form on the surface of a stretch ceiling. The material is antibacterial and non-hygroscopic — it doesn't absorb moisture.

### Chlorine resistance

For pool halls and wellness areas, chlorine resistance is essential. Stretch ceilings offer this resistance as standard.

## Design options for bathrooms

A bathroom doesn't have to be a boring white room. With a stretch ceiling, you have several interesting options:

### Sky-print ceiling

Imagine lying in the bathtub and looking up at a blue sky with clouds. A translucent ceiling with photo print and LED backlighting creates this effect perfectly. It's one of our most popular solutions for bathrooms.

### Glossy surface for visual enlargement

Small bathroom? A glossy (highly reflective) stretch ceiling visually enlarges the room thanks to its mirror effect. It reflects light and the space below, creating an impression of greater height.

### Integrated LED lighting

Recessed spot lights or LED strips directly in the ceiling. Clean design with no visible fixtures. In a bathroom, this is not only aesthetic but also practical: no fixture to fog up.

## Frequently asked questions

### Can a stretch ceiling handle hot steam from the shower?

Yes. The film is designed for temperatures from −40 °C to +80 °C. Normal shower steam won't affect it in any way.

### How do you clean a stretch ceiling in a bathroom?

Just wipe occasionally with a damp cloth or mild cleaning agent. No special maintenance is needed.

### Can I have spot lights in the bathroom ceiling?

Of course. Spot lights, LED strips and even chandeliers can be integrated into a stretch ceiling in the bathroom. We use lights with appropriate IP ratings (IP44 and above).

### What if my neighbour floods my flat?

A stretch ceiling can hold up to **100 litres of water per 1 m²** of surface. The film bulges like a balloon but doesn't let the water through. A technician then safely drains the water and the ceiling returns to its original shape.
